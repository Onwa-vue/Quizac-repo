A sound wave travels through a metal rod with wavelength λ and frequency f. Which of the following best describes the wave when it passes into the surrounding air?

Points: 10
Duration: 30s
Difficulty: Easy
Less λ Equal f
Less λ Less f
Greater λ Equal f
Greater λ Less f
Greater λ Greater f
Equal λ Equal f
Less λ Less f
Less λ Greater f

Review

Sounds travel faster in solids than through gases. Therefore, if the sound moves from the metal rod to air, its speed decreases. But its frequency does not change. Since it must fulfil v = λf then the wavelength must decrease.
